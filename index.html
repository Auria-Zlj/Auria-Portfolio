X-Heal | ACL Rehab System

ⓘ
ACL — anterior cruciate ligament, a key knee ligament often injured in sports.

The Challenge

After ACL surgery, patients often find it difficult to perform exercises correctly without real-time guidance. Clinicians can only check progress during infrequent visits, making it hard to assess recovery quality or form. Over time, this lack of feedback leads to frustration, loss of motivation, and slower recovery.

We asked “How might we help patients recover confidently at home, while giving clinicians real-time visibility into their progress?”
Goal Make ACL recovery measurable, motivating, and connected.

To address these challenges, we followed an iterative design process that bridged user empathy, data-driven design, and system prototyping.

Research & Insights

Introduction

We started by investigating how ACL patients perform rehabilitation exercises at home and how clinicians evaluate their progress. Our goal was to uncover barriers in motivation, accuracy, and communication that impact recovery outcomes.

Research Methods

To capture both human experience and clinical context, we combined primary and secondary research.

Method Source Objective
User Interviews 3 ACL patients · 2 physical therapists Understand daily rehab routines, pain points, and emotional barriers
Secondary Research Hospital protocols, PT guidelines, academic literature Identify clinical constraints and best practices in post-ACL therapy
Competitive Benchmarking Existing rehab apps & wearable systems Analyze limitations in current feedback and monitoring solutions
Problem Landscape

Through this research, we discovered that ACL recovery challenges go beyond movement itself — they reflect a broken feedback loop between patients and clinicians. Rehabilitation begins in the clinic but continues in isolation at home. Without real-time feedback, patients struggle to stay confident while clinicians lack visibility into progress, leaving both sides uncertain about recovery outcomes.

Key Findings

Our research revealed two sides of the same problem patients lack guidance and motivation, while clinicians lack visibility and data accuracy.

Patients

  • Struggle to perform exercises correctly without guidance
  • Lose motivation when progress feels invisible
  • Can’t distinguish between pain and improvement

Clinicians

  • Rely on in-person visits and subjective reports
  • Lack real-time visibility into home exercises
  • Need faster, reliable digital reports
Together, these insights revealed a broken feedback loop — what patients do isn’t visible, what clinicians know isn’t current, leaving both sides uncertain about true progress.

Design Goal

We defined four experience pillars to guide both system architecture and user experience — connecting motivation, feedback, and clinical reliability.

Clinical Reference — Standard ACL Rehab Movements

To ensure our sensing logic aligned with clinical practice, we analyzed standard ACL rehabilitation protocols from hospital physiotherapy guidelines.

These movements informed sensor placement, angle thresholds, and distance-based detection logic used in our BLE system.

Exercise Type Description Key Parameters
Hip Flexion Leg lifted while lying down, hold for 6s Target: ~30cm, 180° alignment
Hamstring Curl Supine curl to engage knee flexor Range: ~90°
Heel Raise Standing vertical motion Heel lift 2.5–5 cm
With clinically grounded goals defined, we ideated the connected X-Heal system — bridging human motivation, clinical oversight, and technical feasibility through an integrated architecture.

Ideate

System Ideation — Closing the Feedback Loop

To translate our design goals into a connected solution, we mapped how clinicians, patients, and data interact across the rehabilitation process. The X-Heal system integrates BLE sensors, cloud processing, and real-time dashboards to form a continuous feedback cycle between users and technology.

Each interaction is supported by measurable data — ensuring patients receive timely feedback, and clinicians gain live visibility for adaptive decision-making.

System Highlights

BLE Sensor Layer

Captures joint angle and leg height in real time.

Cloud Layer (Firebase)

Streams and syncs session data for analysis and report generation.

Dashboard Layer

Enables clinician monitoring, smart alerts, and adaptive plan updates.

User Layer

Provides guided feedback and visual progress to sustain motivation.

Data Flow — From Motion to Meaning

To enable accurate, responsive feedback, we established a data pipeline that processes BLE sensor input into actionable rehabilitation insights.

This pipeline transformed raw sensor signals into meaningful recovery feedback — closing the loop between movement, motivation, and clinical oversight.

Prototype — Bringing the System to Life

Hardware Prototype

We designed and assembled dual-sensor modules integrating BLE microcontrollers, custom PCBs, and 3D-printed enclosures for leg and base positioning.
The hardware prototype ensured reliable motion capture and comfortable user fit during exercises.

From hardware to interaction — translating data into human experience.

System Integration

BLE sensors stream angle and height data to Firebase in real time. Data is processed through calibration logic and visualized instantly in the web interface, enabling closed-loop feedback between patients and clinicians.

UI Prototype

The React-based web interface provides real-time visual feedback, tracking accuracy, hold duration, and rep count. Patients receive motivational feedback, while clinicians access automated progress reports.

Physical Prototype

The enclosure was 3D-printed and refined for comfort and sensor stability. The final prototype was tested for alignment, durability, and usability during rehab sessions.

Together, these prototypes translated the X-Heal system into a tangible, testable experience — merging hardware precision, software intelligence, and human-centered design.

Outcome & Impact

  • Increased patient confidence and consistency.
  • Enabled remote supervision and data-driven insights for clinicians.
  • Reduced motion feedback latency from minutes to milliseconds.
  • Prototype successfully integrated into the CareLink+ 5G test environment.

Reflection

Designing for healthcare required balancing precision and empathy. We learned to validate not just technical accuracy, but emotional reassurance—because recovery isn't only about movement; it's about trust.
